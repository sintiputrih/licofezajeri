Marcos Rojo is an Argentine professional footballer who is best known for his career as a defender for Boca Juniors and the Argentina national team. He has also played for teams like Estudiantes, Spartak Moscow, Manchester United, and Sporting CP in the past. During the course of his career, Marcos has won several tournaments such as Copa Libertadores, Argentine Primera División, EFL Cup, FA Cup, UEFA Europa League, FA Community Shield, Copa Argentina, and Copa de la Liga Profesional.

Marcos Rojo Facts

  • At the age of 10, Rojo became a member of Estudiantes de La Plata.
  • In 2009, Marcos was promoted to the first team of Estudiantes de La Plata.
  • In the 2009 FIFA Club World Cup Final, as a member of Estudiantes, Rojo faced FC Barcelona. Eventually, the Spanish team took the trophy after they won the match by 2-1.
  • He won the Copa Libertadores and Argentine’s First League title while playing with Estudiantes.
  • In December 2010, Marcos penned a five-year deal with the Russian club Spartak Moscow.
  • In July 2012, Rojo shifted to Sporting Lisbon and signed a four-year contract for a price of 3.5 million British pounds.
  • He scored his first goal for Sporting Lisbon on April 28, 2013, in a match against Nacional.
  • On August 19, 2014, Marcos moved to Manchester United and penned a five-year contract worth 20 million euros. At the same time, Nani shifted to Sporting on a season-long loan. After Rojo got transferred to Manchester, he stated that this was a dream come true for him.
  • He played for Argentina at the 2014 FIFA World Cup.
  • Rojo has tattoos with the words “Pride” and “Glory” on the right and left sides of his quadricep (leg) respectively.
  • In December 2014, Rojo was part of an affair with another woman whom he had met in a club. The Argentinian superstar stated that the girl was blackmailing him. Eventually, the injunction was removed in April 2015, but it is still valid to stop the publishing of photos of Rojo.

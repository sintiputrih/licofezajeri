Back to School: Tips on how to save on supplies

Evaluate What You Have

Before rushing out to buy new supplies, take stock of what you already have at home. Check your drawers, cabinets, and closets for any unused or leftover school items from the previous year. You might find that you already have plenty of pens, notebooks, and other essentials.

Create a Budget

Setting a budget is crucial to ensure that you don’t overspend during the back-to-school shopping frenzy. Determine how much you can afford to spend and allocate amounts for different categories, such as notebooks, stationery, backpacks, and clothing.

Make a List and Stick to It

Compile a comprehensive list of the items you need and stick to it when you’re shopping. This will help you avoid impulse purchases and stay focused on the essentials.

Shop the Sales

Keep an eye on back-to-school sales from various stores. Many retailers offer significant discounts on supplies during this time. Shopping during sales can lead to substantial savings on items like backpacks, folders, and art supplies.

Buy in Bulk

Consider buying supplies in bulk, especially items that you know you’ll use throughout the school year. This approach often results in lower costs per unit and helps you stock up for the future.

Comparison Shop Online

Take advantage of online shopping to compare prices across different retailers. Online platforms make it easy to find the best deals on specific items and save money on gas and transportation.

Utilize Coupons and Promo Codes

Search for coupons and promo codes before making any online or in-store purchases. These codes can provide extra discounts that add up to significant savings.

Consider Secondhand Options

Explore secondhand stores or online marketplaces for gently used school items. Items like backpacks, textbooks, and clothing can often be found at a fraction of the cost of new ones.

DIY Supplies

Get creative and make some of your supplies yourself. Personalized notebooks, pencil cases, and locker decorations can be fun DIY projects that save you money.

Wait for Tax-Free Weekends

Many states offer tax-free weekends during the back-to-school season. Take advantage of these weekends to buy items like clothing, shoes, and school supplies without paying sales tax.

Shop Throughout the Year

Instead of doing all your shopping right before school starts, spread it out over the course of the year. This approach gives you more time to find deals and reduces the financial burden during a single month.

Take Advantage of Student Discounts

If you’re a college student, make sure to use your student ID to access discounts on various supplies and services. Many retailers and companies offer special deals for students.

Set Limits with Your Kids

If you’re shopping with children, set clear limits on what they can choose. This will help avoid unnecessary purchases and teach them about responsible spending.

Prioritize Quality over Brand Names

While brand-name products might be tempting, prioritize quality over labels. Generic or store-brand items can often be just as good as their more expensive counterparts.

Reuse and Recycle

Encourage your children to reuse items from the previous school year, such as binders and calculators. Additionally, recycle items like paper and plastic to contribute to a sustainable lifestyle.

James Lee Burke Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

James Lee Burke (born December 5, 1936) is an American mystery novelist who is best known for his Dave Robicheaux series of novels. He has received two Edgar Awards for his novels Black Cherry Blues (1990) and Cimarron Rose (2001). (1998). In film, the Robicheaux role has been played by two different actors: first by Alec Baldwin (Heaven’s Prisoners) and later by Tommy Lee Jones (The Departed) (In the Electric Mist). Furthermore, Burke has authored seven miscellaneous crime novels, two short storey collections, four books featuring protagonist Texas attorney Billy Bob Holland, and three books starring Billy Bob’s cousin and fellow law enforcement officer Texas sheriff Hackberry Holland.

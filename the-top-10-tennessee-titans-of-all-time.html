The Top 10 Tennessee Titans of All-Time

The Tennessee Titans, formerly known as the Houston Oilers, have a rich history in the NFL, and their roster has seen some remarkable talent over the years. 

1. Steve McNair

Steve McNair is revered as the only true franchise quarterback in the history of the Tennessee Titans. Known as "Air McNair," he led the team to the playoffs four times and was selected to three Pro Bowls.

He threw 156 touchdowns and rushed for 36 more during his illustrious 10-year career with the Titans.

McNair's legacy is further immortalized by the fact that members of the Tennessee Titans wore a No. 9 sticker on their helmets in the season following his tragic murder in 2009.

2. Warren Moon

Warren Moon holds the record for the most passing yards in franchise history with an impressive 33,685 yards during his time with the Tennessee Titans. His tenure with the team, including his leadership and high-powered offense, has solidified his place as one of the greatest players in the franchise's history.

3. Keith Bulluck

Keith Bulluck, a three-time All-Pro, is widely regarded as the best defensive player in the history of the Tennessee Titans. His remarkable stats include 770 tackles, 15 forced fumbles, 69 defended passes, 19 interceptions, and 18 sacks during his tenure with the Titans.

4. Blaine Bishop

Bishop was a hard-hitting, tackling machine, who amassed 517 total tackles and 12 forced fumbles during his time with the Tennessee Titans. He was a four-time Pro Bowler and a three-time All-Pro, providing veteran leadership to the Titans' defense.

5. Frank Wycheck

Wycheck's contribution to the Titans is perhaps most famously remembered for his lateral pass to Kevin Dyson in the "Music City Miracle," a historic play that led the Titans to their first Super Bowl berth.

6. Michael Roos

As the best player who spent his entire career with the Titans, Michael Roos has solidified his place as one of the top left tackles in the league. With one Pro Bowl selection and three All-Pro honors, Roos has been a cornerstone of the Titans' offensive line.

7. Akeem Ayers

Ayers, a promising young talent, showcased his potential during his time with the Titans. Although his career was cut short, his impact on the team was significant.

8. Jurrell Casey

Casey's tenure with the Titans saw him emerging as a promising defensive force, demonstrating his potential to become a franchise player for the team.

9. Kendall Wright

Wright's time with the Titans was marked by his potential to become a key player for the team. His contribution to the team's offense was notable, showcasing his promise as a talented wide receiver.

10. Zach Brown

Zach Brown, known for his potential and promising performances, contributed significantly to the Titans' defense during his time with the team.

 

The Tennessee Titans have been home to some of the most iconic players in NFL history.

The impact of these players has not only shaped the franchise's legacy but also left an indelible mark on the sport as a whole.

Their contributions have played a pivotal role in the success and growth of the Tennessee Titans, making them an integral part of the team's storied history.

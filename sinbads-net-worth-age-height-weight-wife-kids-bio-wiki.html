Early Life

David Adkins was born in 1956 on 10th November to a Baptist Dr. Donald Beckley Adkins and Louise Adkins. He has two sisters named Dorothea and Donna, and three brothers named Donald, Michael, and Mark.

Sinbad completed his graduation from Benton Harbor High School. Later he graduated from the University of Denver, where he also lettered for two seasons with the college basketball team.

Personal Life

He has married twice to the same person twice in his life. He first married his wife Meredith Adkins in 1985; however, the couple separated in 1992.

Later the two rekindled their love in early 2002 and got married by the end of the year. The couple has two kids together.

He likes to keep his personal life very private, and since the whole Tax case scandal, he has made sure to keep his lay low-key in the entertainment business.

Career

He entered the world of comedy in 1978. He became so big during the 1980s that he was being considered as the most influential stand-up comedian of all time.

Sinbad has also appeared in several movies like ‘Good Burger,’ ‘First Kid,’ ‘Planes,’ ‘The Cherokee,’ ‘The Meteor Man,’ ‘Coneheads,’ ‘Necessary Roughness,’ ‘’Crazy as Hell, ‘Stompin’’, and ‘Club Med.’

Most of his stand-ups revolved around genres like Observational comedy, black comedy, political satire, character comedy pop music, and surreal humor.

He also makes satires around subjects like Everyday life, parenting, self-deprecation, marriage, American politics, friend, pop culture, current events, family, race relations, relationships, aging, and racism.

Sinbad is also popular for his ability to work with music; he can play multiple musical instruments and made the most of his talent, by using them in his shows. He has also played for several artists like Memphis Red and Journell Henry (J. Henry).

Awards & Achievements

Sinbad was nominated for the NAACP Image Award in the category of ‘Outstanding Performance in an Animated/Live-Action/Dramatic Youth or Children’s Series/Special’ for his work in 1996 ‘Happily Ever After Fairy Tales for Every Child.’

In 2009, he was also ranked in the ten worst tax debtor cases, in which he owed over $2.5 million to the government of California.

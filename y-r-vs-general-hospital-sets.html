    Acadia_ca sats: I read in a comment on this board at some point within the last year, that the Young and Restless moved stages and their new stage area is much smaller than the previous one. Whether there’s truth to that or not I don’t know.

    In 2018, CBS sold Television City to the real estate investment company Hackman Capital Partners while continuing to lease exclusive Y&R production space. Y&R didn’t move to a smaller stage, they are leasing fewer stage sets to produce Show.

    A year before, 2017, Sony/CBS acquired Y&R from the Bell family. The Bell’s relinquished control of Y&R: advertising dollars are paid to Sony/CBS, Bell Dramatic Serial, Inc./Corday Productions before the Show production company, subsequently there were cost cutting measures instituted; fewer sets and cheaper decor (few characters have home sets, hotel room set repurposed for several character’s use), protracted contract negotiations, legacy characters let go (Paul Williams, Cane and the Twins, Esther, etc) or go on extended leave (Nina, Kevin, Ashley), killed off (Rey) or recast with unknown actors (Chance, Summer).

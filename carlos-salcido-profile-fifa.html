Carlos Salcido Profile, Height, Wife, Salary, FIFA, Family

Carlos Salcido is an international Mexican football team player. He was born on 2 April 1980 and his birthplace is Ocotlan, Jalisco, Mexico. Salcido has played in the Guadalajara, PSV, Fulham, and Tigres UANL club and also Mexico national football team.

Generally, his playing position is Defender. In truth, Salcido is an important footballer on the team. His height is 5 feet 9 ½ inches. His full name is Carlos Arnoldo Salcido Flores.

Carlos Salcido club and international career

Mexican team footballer Salcido began his career at his nearby home team Chivas. On 10 July 2006, he signed a four-year contract with Dutch Champions PSV Eindhoven. And he scored his first goal in Europe, a spectacular long-distance goal in a match with Excelsior Rotterdam that ended 4-0 on 18 November 2006.

After their best performance with Mexico when he joined the FIFA World Cup 2010. It was announced that Fulham had agreed to a 3.6 Million EUR deal with PSV to sign the player on 26 August 2010. Besides, he made his debut with Blackburn Rovers, on 18 September 2010. It was announced that he was transferred to Mexican club Tigres UANL on 17 August 2011.

Fulham confirmed that Salcido had joined Tigres on a permanent basis on 7 March 2012. Afterward, he returned to the Guadalajara club on 20 May 2014. Besides, he scored his first goal against Bitter rivals America with a penalty shot on 24 November 2016.

He played in the Confederations Cup in Germany, helping the Mexico team to a fourth-place finish in 2005. Salcido played all four matches during the FIFA World Cup 2006 in Germany and he has played in the 2006 World Cup season.

He was called up by the Mexican international Hugo Sanchez for the Gold Cup 2007. And then he played in the semifinals of the Gold Cup 2007 on 21 June.

Moreover, he played all four matches in South Africa at the World Cup 2010. Also, he was included in’ Mexico’s FIFA World Cup 2014 was a surprise to the player. On 29 August 2014, Salcido proclaimed his retirement from the international football team.

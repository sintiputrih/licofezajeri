Mario Casas – Career and Professional Life

Mario Casas began appearing in soap operas from the year 2005 at the age of 19. Then, he started earning minor roles in several Spanish films. He began his acting career with episode appearances in the television series Obsesion (English: Obsession), Motivos personales (Personal Motives), and Mujeres (Women). And, he came to domestic media attention through his film debut in the year 2006 film “El Camino de Los Ingleses (Summer Rain)”. It was directed by Antonio Banderas.

In the same year, he joined the cast of the LaSexta series “SMS”. He then reached an international audience as Aitor Carrasco in the Antena 3 series “Los hombres de Paco (Paco’s Men)”. He starred in it from the year 2007 to 2010. Then, in the year 2009, he appeared in two hit films, “Fuga deCerebross (Brain Drain)” opposite Amaia Salamanca, and “Mentiras y Gordas (Sex, Party and Lies)” along with his best friends Yon Gonzalez, Ana Maria Polvorosa, Ana de Armas,  and Hugo Silva.

Likewise, both films managed to lead the box office over the weekend of their release. And then, in the year  2010, Casas starred in “Carne de Neon (Neon Meat)” directed by Paco Cabezas. Later that year in December, he starred in “Tres metros Sobre elCieloo (Three Steps Above Heaven)” opposite Maria Valverde. The film was eventually the highest-grossing Spanish film of the year.

A sequel “Tengo ganas de ti (I Want You)”, which stars him, Valverde, and Clara Lago, premiered in the year 2012. He also appeared in “Miedo” in the year 2010. And Jaume Balaguero directed it.

Furthermore, Casas’s latest role is “El fotografo de Mauthausen” from the year 2018. It tells the story of Francisco Boi, a Spanish Republican photographer who was sent to the Mauthausen-Gusen concentration camp complex during World War II. Moreover, he lost around 12 kilograms (26 pounds) to play Boix during his imprisonment.

Awards

Mario Casas has won many awards during his career. He received the “Association of Latin Entertainment Critics Award” for “Best New Actor” for “Three Steps Above Heaven” in 2011. Likewise, in 2009 he received “EnE Film Awards” as the “Best Actor in a Supporting Role” for “Sex, Lies, and Parties”. In the same year for the same movie, he won “EP3 Awards” for “Best New Actor” and “LesGaiCineMad Festival Awards” for the “LesGaiCineMad Award for Acting”.

Moreover, he won “Zaragoza Film Festival” for “Best Young Talent ” in 2009 and 2010 for Brain Drain
Sex, Lies, Parties, and Three Steps Above Heaven. Likewise, he won “Micrófono de OroAwards” in 2011 for “Best Television Actor” for Paco’s Men and The Boat. Also, he received “Neox Fan Award” in 2012 for “Best Film Actor” for the movie, I Want You. Last but not least, he received the “Villarreal International Short Film Festival” in 2011 for “Best Actor” for “Easy Money”.

Furthermore, he was also nominated for the Alicante Film Festival, Fotogramas de Plata Awards, Móstoles International Short Film Festival Awards, Must! Awards, New Talent of Spanish Cinema Awards, and Shanghai Awards as well. These were all from the year 2009-2012.

What is Andy Reid's salary?

Andy Reid, the highly successful head coach of the Kansas City Chiefs, has had a remarkable coaching career spanning over two decades.

Andy Reid's Current Contract and Salary

As of the most recent information available, Andy Reid is believed to earn roughly $12.5 million per season.

His current net worth is estimated to be between $20 million and $30 million, positioning him among the highest-paid coaches in the NFL. In 2020, Reid inked a six-year agreement with the Chiefs, tying him to Kansas City through the 2025 season.

Despite his extensive career, Reid shows no signs of retiring anytime soon and has expressed his openness to coaching well into his seventies.

Salary History and Extensions

Reid initially signed a five-year deal to be the head coach of the Chiefs in 2013 and signed his first extension in the summer of 2017.

Notably, in 2020, the same year he and the Chiefs won Super Bowl LIV, Reid was extended through the 2025 season.

The exact figure of Reid's salary remains undisclosed to the public, but it is reported that he is making about $10 million per year, and based on the extension, this translates to earning somewhere in the ballpark of $60 million in total.

This extension came after the Chiefs achieved significant success under Reid's leadership, winning their division seven times and seeing the postseason nine times.

Bonus and Incentives

While specific details about bonus structures and incentives in Reid's contract are not widely available, it is common for high-profile coaches like Reid to have performance-based bonuses tied to their contracts.

Given his exceptional track record and the Chiefs' consistent success under his guidance, it is reasonable to assume that his contract includes various performance incentives and bonuses linked to playoff appearances, division titles, and potentially Super Bowl victories.

Andy Reid's coaching career has been synonymous with success, with his leadership guiding the Kansas City Chiefs to new heights in the NFL.

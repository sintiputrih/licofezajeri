Owen Orser Accident: Know More About this Incident

In a tragic incident, a young man named Owen Orser lost his life in a morning car crash in Bucks County. This article provides further details about the unfortunate event, aiming to shed light on what happened.

Who Was Owen Orser?

Owen Orser, known as a skilled mechanic at Bergey’s Truck Centers, attended Pennridge High School. He resided in Perkasie, Pennsylvania, and had his roots in the same town. Remembered for his kind and compassionate nature, Owen was always willing to lend a hand and provide assistance. His authenticity and genuine soul left a lasting impression on those who knew him. His demeanor reflected kindness, confidence, and consideration towards others.

Owen Orser Accident: How It Happened

A car veered off the road and hit a tree at approximately 4 in the morning on Monday. This happened in the 1700 block of Richlandtown Pike, close to Keystone Road, in Springfield Township. The driver, Owen Orser, who was 23 years old, was taken to the hospital. Sadly, he was declared deceased about an hour later. The coroner of Bucks County, Meredith Buck, shared this information. The man, who lived in Hilltown Township, had his death determined to be an accident, according to the authorities.

The state police mentioned that Orser was driving too quickly given the road conditions, especially while navigating a curve. Tragically, he was not wearing a seat belt at the time. It’s worth noting that the road was wet during this incident.

Susan Peters-Dalbey expressed her sentiments in a Facebook post, sharing,

“This guy, always with a smile,always a great friend to all. Such a kind soul. I am so thankful for the time we had with you.Owen Orser you were like a son to Rob and I. We are devastated. You were to young to go to Heaven. We will miss you, Owen. Until we meet again.”

– Wrote by Susan Peters-Dalbey

Obituary And Funeral Arrangements

The family of Owen Orser will release his obituary and details about the funeral arrangements in due time. They are currently in a process of healing and need some time before they are ready to share information about his funeral plans.
